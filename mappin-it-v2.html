ALL ABOUT MAPPIN' IT

Mappin' It is a communally crowdsourced digital map of Singapore for Migrant Domestic Workers (MDWs) to share about the different places in Singapore they frequent and what it means to them.

MDWs, MDW organisations and non-MDW members of the public can "Place A Pin" on a specific location on the map. Under the pin, users can write a short note and/or upload photos and videos about the place to share more about their experience of that specific place.

This website started as a student-led initiative by a group of 4 students from the National University of Singapore's Tembusu College, under the course UTS2115 Migrant Workers, Rhetoric and Performance.

Our mission is to amplify the voices of MDWs in Singapore by creating a living, breathing archive of the spaces they call their own — places of joy, community, and belonging in a city far from home.

HOW TO USE MAPPIN' IT

A friendly step-by-step guide to navigating our interactive map

🗺️ Navigating the Map

1
Zoom in & out using your mouse scroll wheel, trackpad pinch, or the + / − buttons at the bottom-left of the map. The map is restricted to Singapore, so you'll always stay on the island.
2
Pan and drag the map by clicking and dragging to explore different parts of Singapore — from Jurong to Changi, Orchard to Woodlands.
3
At the overview level, you'll see three key locations labelled on the map: Lucky Plaza, Peninsula Plaza, and City Plaza. These are popular gathering spots for MDWs. Click their labels or pins to zoom in.

📍 Understanding the Pins

Different coloured pins represent different categories of experience. Here's what each colour means:

🔴 General Location
🟡 Food
🩷 Shopping
🟢 Social
🟣 Fun Activities
🔵 Resources
🟠 Organisation (Archive)
⚪ Moderator Fact (Archive)

👁️ Viewing Contributions

1
Click on any coloured pin on the map to open the contributions panel on the left side of the screen. You'll see a list of posts from other users about that specific category and location.
2
Click the › arrow next to any contribution to open its full detail — including photos, videos, and the full written story.
3
Users with a 🔵 blue tick are verified members. Users marked (Public) have contributed without a registered account.

✏️ Placing a Pin

1
Zoom into the area of Singapore you'd like to pin.
2
Click on the map at your chosen spot. A red pin will drop to mark your selected location.
3
Click the "PLACE A PIN" button at the bottom-right corner of the screen.
4
In the form that appears, choose a category for your pin (Food, Shopping, Social, Fun Activities, Resources, etc.).
5
Upload photos or videos from your device to bring your contribution to life. Then add a title and write about your experience.
6
Click "PLACE A PIN" to publish. Your pin will appear on the map and be clickable by everyone!

🗃️ The Archive

1
Click Archive in the top navigation bar to switch to the archive view. Only orange and white pins are shown here.
2
Orange pins are from verified MDW organisations (like HOME, HOME ROSES, and Kaugnay). They share information about past and current events, initiatives, and campaigns.
3
White pins are moderator contributions — "Did You Know?" facts about MDW working conditions, rights, and life in Singapore.

👤 Your Profile

1
Click your name in the top-right corner to visit your profile page. Here you can see all the pins you've contributed.
2
Click the › arrow on any of your contributions to view its full details on the right side.
3
Click the Edit button on any of your contributions to update the title, body text, or media (photos/videos).
4
Click on your profile picture to open the avatar editor — choose an emoji avatar, upload a photo, or remove your picture entirely.
